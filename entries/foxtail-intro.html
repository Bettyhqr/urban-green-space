URBAN GREEN
SPACE

Name
Green Foxtail, known scientifically as "Setaria viridis", belongs to Poaceae.
Features
Appearance: Distinctive, cylindrical seed heads that resemble a foxtail, turning from green to brown as they mature. Scent: a mild, grassy scent, especially when crushed. Uses: Often used as forage for livestock and can be beneficial for wildlife. Season: Grows spring to fall, flowers in summer to early autumn.
Where I found it
I found Green Foxtail near the riverbank in New York City's Hudson River Park. This hardy grass thrives in urban environments, adding greenery to the park's waterfront.

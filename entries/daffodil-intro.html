URBAN GREEN
SPACE

Name
Hydrangea, known scientifically as "Hydrangea macrophylla," belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family.
Features
Appearance: Hydrangeas are known for their large, rounded flower clusters that come in shades of blue, pink, white, and purple. Scent: Hydrangeas are mostly unscented, with a very faint floral smell. Uses: Hydrangeas are often grown in gardens for their ornamental value and used in bouquets and floral arrangements. Season: Bloom from late spring to early autumn.
Where I found it
I found hydrangeas in the West Village, growing in small urban gardens and around brownstone entrances.
